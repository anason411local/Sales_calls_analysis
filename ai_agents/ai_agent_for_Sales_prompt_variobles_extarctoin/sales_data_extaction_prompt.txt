SALES CALL ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK - DATA EXTRACTION PROMPT
 
This framework is designed to extract and analyze key data points from sales call transcripts without scoring. The focus is on identifying specific patterns, behaviors, and outcomes that provide insight into call performance.
 
================================================================================
CATEGORY I: CUSTOMER ENGAGEMENT & INTEREST (15% Total Weightage)
================================================================================
 
Description: This category evaluates the depth and quality of customer participation in the conversation, measuring both quantitative engagement metrics and qualitative indicators of interest or resistance.
 
I.1 Customer vs Agent Talk Ratio (40% of Category Weightage):
 
Description: Measures the percentage breakdown of conversation between the customer and the sales representative to determine if the dialogue is mutual, agent-heavy, or customer-heavy.
 
Data to Extract:
- Total number of agent turns/utterances
- Total number of customer turns/utterances
- Percentage of conversation by agent (%)
- Percentage of conversation by customer (%)
- Classification: [Mutual Dialogue / Agent-Heavy / Customer-Heavy]
- Agent Verbiage: Extract representative sample of agent's speaking patterns
- Customer Verbiage: Extract representative sample of customer's speaking patterns
 
Analysis Notes: A mutual dialogue typically shows 40-60% customer participation. Agent-heavy conversations (customer <30%) may indicate monologuing. Customer-heavy conversations (customer >70%) may indicate loss of control.
 
---
 
I.2 Discovery Questions Asked Before Disengage (30% of Category Weightage):
 
Description: Counts and categorizes the number and quality of discovery questions the agent asks before the customer disengages or the call concludes.
 
Data to Extract:
 
Goal 1 - Demographics / Operations Questions (Q1-Q9):
- How long have you been in business? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Do you service residential, commercial, or both? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Do you service within a 50-mile radius or more? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- What are your top 5 services that you provide? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Are you a one-man team or do you have employees? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Do you do mobile work, have a shop, or both? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- What kind of vehicle/equipment do you have? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- What made you decide to open up your own business? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Total Goal 1 Questions Asked: [Count]
 
Goal 2 - Competition / Marketing Questions (Q1-Q6):
- Do you currently have a website? How effective is it? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Are you managing marketing yourself or is someone managing it? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- What type of advertising are you doing? (Facebook, directories, print) [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- How much do you spend on advertising monthly? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- What services are you getting for that price? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- How do non-referral prospects find you? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Total Goal 2 Questions Asked: [Count]
 
Goal 3 - Practical Need / Mindset Questions (Q7-Q10):
- When was the last time you used Google to look for a service? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Were you able to find what you were looking for quickly? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- How often do you see people using phones to find things? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Do you know anyone who uses online/Google to look for something? [Yes/No - Timestamp]
- Total Goal 3 Questions Asked: [Count]
 
Advanced Discovery - "Peeling the Onion":
- Did agent ask initial open-ended pain point question? [Yes/No]
- Initial Question Verbiage: [Extract exact quote]
- Did agent probe deeper with follow-up questions? [Yes/No]
- Probing Question Verbiage: [Extract exact quotes]
- Example: "What are your biggest challenges in attracting new customers online?"
 
Total Discovery Questions: [Sum of all goals]
Quality Assessment: [Surface-Level / Moderate Depth / Deep Discovery]

CATEGORY II: CALL OPENING & FRAMING (10% Total Weightage)
================================================================================
 
Description: This category evaluates how effectively the agent establishes the call's purpose, context, and structure within the opening moments.
 
II.1 Time to State Clear Reason for Call (40% of Category Weightage):
 
Description: Measures how quickly (in seconds) the agent communicates the specific reason for the call after the initial greeting.
 
Data to Extract:
- Initial greeting timestamp: [MM:SS]
- Reason for call stated timestamp: [MM:SS]
- Time elapsed: [Seconds]
- Reason for call verbiage: [Extract exact quote]
- Was reason stated clearly? [Yes/No]
- Agent Verbiage: [Complete opening statement through reason for call]
 
Example Pattern: "I'm calling, John, to discuss how 411 Locals can help your plumbing business in Anytown..."
 
---
 
II.2 Business Type & Location Referenced in First 30-45 Seconds (30% of Category Weightage):
 
Description: Identifies whether the agent personalizes the opening by mentioning the prospect's specific business type and location within the first 30-45 seconds.
 
Data to Extract:

- Location mentioned: [Yes/No - Timestamp - Verbiage]
- Within first 30 seconds? [Yes/No]
- Within first 45 seconds? [Yes/No]
- Personalization quality: [Generic / Specific / Highly Tailored]

Example Pattern: "I noticed you're a plumbing business in Anytown..."
 
---
 
II.3 Clarity of Call Structure Communicated Up Front (30% of Category Weightage):
 
Description: Assesses whether the agent frames what will happen during the call, setting clear expectations for the conversation flow.
 
Data to Extract:
- Call structure mentioned: [Yes/No - Timestamp]

================================================================================
OUTPUT FORMAT REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================
 
All extracted data should be compiled into a CSV format with the following structure:
 
Column Headers:
- Category
- Sub-Category
- Data Point Name
- Value/Finding
- Timestamp (if applicable)
- Agent Verbiage
- Customer Verbiage
- Notes/Context
 
Special Instructions:
1. Extract exact verbatim quotes - do not paraphrase or summarize
2. All timestamps should be in MM:SS format
3. For Yes/No fields, provide timestamp and verbiage when Yes
4. For count fields, provide detailed breakdown
5. Mark N/A when data point is not applicable or not found
6. Include context notes for ambiguous situations
7. Ensure each row represents a distinct, unique data point
8. Do not duplicate verbiage across multiple data points
 
CSV Export Guidelines:
- Use proper escaping for quotes within verbiage fields
- Separate multiple instances with semicolon (;) within cells
- Keep verbiage excerpts under 200 characters per cell (use "..." for truncation)
- Include full transcript references in Notes column if truncated
 
================================================================================
ANALYSIS RULES (STRICT)
================================================================================
 
1. Do not assume or infer anything outside the transcript
2. Do not interpret sentiment - extract actual language used
3. Do not modify or paraphrase customer/agent verbiage
4. Count all instances explicitly - do not estimate
5. Mark all timestamps relative to call start (00:00)
6. If a data point cannot be determined, state "Not Found in Transcript"
7. If a stage is skipped, explicitly note it as "Skipped"
8. Extract both quantitative (counts, times) and qualitative (verbiage, patterns) data
9. Cross-reference related data points for consistency
10. Note any unusual patterns or anomalies in the Notes field
 
================================================================================
FINAL DELIVERABLE
================================================================================
 
The analysis should produce:
 
1. Complete CSV file with all data points extracted
2. Summary statistics for each category
3. Notable patterns or anomalies identified
4. Call flow diagram showing stage progression with timestamps
5. Sentiment progression chart (if applicable)
6. Key insights section highlighting:
   - Strongest areas of performance
   - Weakest areas needing improvement
   - Critical moments that influenced call outcome
   - Recommendations for agent coaching
